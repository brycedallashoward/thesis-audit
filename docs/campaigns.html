Stasis Campaign Analysis

12-month performance review, segmented by audience

April 2025 – March 2026 · Email + SMS

How to read this: Campaigns are grouped by audience segment. Each group has its own baseline metrics. Winners and losers are evaluated relative to their segment’s baseline, not against the full program. A 0.3% click rate is strong on a 60K broad send; the same rate would be weak on a 2K purchaser-only send. Arrow links (↗) open the campaign in Klaviyo.

Key findings

Audience segmentation is the biggest lever

Feb 2026 refined targeting (excluding active subs, recent purchasers, flow profiles) drove 61% opens, $0.031 RPR. Same content to the broad Jan list: 47% opens, $0.015 RPR. October Hightouch control: 41% opens, $0.008 RPR.

June 2025 was the gold standard

Eight campaigns, $46K revenue. ADHD lifestyle angles (“Focus, At What Cost?”, “Stop Cancelling Your Plans”) drove 3–5x higher conversion than educational or product content. $0.22 RPR avg vs. $0.04 overall.

Purchaser splits are a 17x multiplier

The 3/7 DST Reset split: purchasers ($0.489 RPR, 0.54% conv) vs. non-purchasers ($0.028 RPR, 0.022% conv). Same content, same day. More campaigns should split by purchase history.

Stasis Kids drives outsized revenue

Two Kids sends generated $15,974 — 26% of Q1 2026 campaign revenue. 0.34% conversion from 53K recipients on the Announcement. 7.49% click from 1.3K waitlist on Back in Stock.

Sale fatigue is measurable

Anniversary Sale: 15 days, 12 sends. BFCM: 26 days, 15+ sends. Unsub rates climbed to 1.27–1.77% on tail-end sends. Cap at 7–10 days, 6–8 sends max.

Plaintext gets 10–20x the click rate

Clinical trial plaintext: 3–4% CTR. Standard designed emails: 0.15–0.30%. A monthly plaintext founder letter could be a low-effort, high-engagement format.
