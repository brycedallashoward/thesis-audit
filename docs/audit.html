3. Biggest Gaps

CRITICAL No Dunning Flow

Subscription Payment Failed shows 0 events. Either the metric is broken or dunning was never configured. For a subscription-first business doing $2M/month, this is a direct revenue leak. Industry standard is 3–5 email/SMS touches + card update CTA.

CRITICAL Winback Converting at 0.21%

45,700 recipients, 48% open rate — people are reading. But 0.21% conversion means the offer, timing, or sequencing isn't working. With ~5,500 cancels/month feeding this flow, every 1% improvement = ~55 recovered subscribers/month = ~$52K ARR.

CRITICAL SMS Absent from Lifecycle

The SMS Welcome Flow proves the channel works ($6.23 RPR, 5.7% conv). But SMS is used nowhere else in the customer journey — no onboarding, no refill reminders, no winback, no dunning. It's a fully built channel that's only being used for promo blasts.

GAP Onboarding Click-Through Crisis

M1 opens at 57%, clicks at 1.3%. M2 opens at 56%, clicks at 1.9%. People are opening and reading — the subject lines and timing are fine. The content inside isn't compelling action. For a product that requires habit formation to retain, this is where churn starts.

GAP No Cross-Sell or OTP Conversion

Stasis Night is being gifted as a retention tactic, but there's no systematic cross-sell flow. OTP customers ($129) have no conversion path to subscription ($59/mo). Two revenue expansion flows that don't exist.

GAP No Subscription Tenure Segmentation

No segments for new sub vs 3mo+ vs 6mo+. No product-specific segments. No LTV tiers. The account is treating all subscribers the same when the data shows clearly different behavioral windows.

4. Strategic Opportunities

OPPORTUNITY The First 25 Days

The refill decision happens around day 20–25. Everything before that is the window to prove the product works. Current onboarding has high opens but low action. Rebuilding M1 onboarding to drive habit formation (usage reminders, expectation setting, early wins) directly attacks the 30–38% monthly churn rate at its root.

OPPORTUNITY SMS as a Lifecycle Channel

SMS Welcome is the highest-RPR flow in the account. Extending SMS into refill reminders, dunning, and winback could be the single highest-leverage channel expansion available. The infrastructure exists — it's just not being used.

OPPORTUNITY Dunning as Immediate Revenue Recovery

Industry averages: a good dunning flow recovers 20–40% of failed payments. If even 5% of the subscriber base has a payment failure each month (~700–850 subscribers), recovering 30% of those at $79/mo = $16–20K/month in recovered revenue. This is the lowest-effort, highest-impact new flow.

OPPORTUNITY Winback Rebuild

5,500 cancels/month feeding into a flow that converts at 0.21%. A rebuilt winback with proper timing, tiered offers, social proof, and SMS could realistically 5–10x that conversion rate. At 1–2% conversion = 55–110 recovered subscribers/month.

OPPORTUNITY Scale Advocacy Programs

Referral and Super User flows have the highest engagement in the account (84% open / 56% click on referrals). They're tiny (632 and 1,400 recipients). Building the pipeline to feed more subscribers into these programs could create a self-reinforcing retention + acquisition loop.

5. 30 / 60 / 90 Day View

First 30 Days — Stop the Bleeding

  • Build and launch dunning flow (email + SMS)
  • Investigate Subscription Payment Failed metric — fix or replace
  • Add SMS to refill reminder flow
  • Clean dead metrics (10 identified) to free up slots
  • Archive dead/draining flows

Days 30–60 — Fix the Foundation

  • Rebuild M1 onboarding for habit formation (usage reminders, expectation management, milestone moments)
  • Rebuild winback flow with tiered offers + SMS
  • Build subscription tenure segments (new / 3mo+ / 6mo+)
  • Launch OTP → subscription conversion flow
  • Add SMS to onboarding sequence

Days 60–90 — Expand

  • Build Stasis cross-sell flow for Thesis subscribers
  • Launch subscription anniversary / milestone flow
  • Scale referral and Super User programs
  • Product-specific onboarding variants (Clarity vs Motivation vs Stress Reset vs Neuroprotection)
  • Campaign strategy shift: introduce active-subscriber education campaigns alongside promo calendar
